Postmortem Analysis

Postmortem Report — Summary Analysis

Coverage & Compilation

32 PM reports compiled across Dharamjaigarh and Raigarh Van Mandals for the period 2021–2026. All available case records have been cross-referenced with the geo-referenced incident database.

Lead Cause — PM Confirmed

Electrocution leading to Cardio-Respiratory Failure confirmed as cause of death in 8 of 32 PM records. Multiple simultaneous electrocution events noted in 3 cases. Drowning is the second-leading confirmed cause (10 of 32).

Age Concern — Neonates & Calves

Neonates and calves (under 1 year) account for approximately 38% of PM cases. Six neonate-specific cases identified. This level of juvenile mortality poses a direct threat to long-term population viability.

Key PM Findings

Five-Year Trend & PM Highlights

  • Sex distribution — 50/50: 16 male / 16 female from 32 records. No sex-biased conflict mortality.
  • Multiple neonates & calves — 6 neonate cases confirmed. Under-1-yr mortality at 38% of PM cases is alarming.
  • 3 simultaneous electrocution events — single incidents killed multiple elephants; indicates systemic power-line hazard.
  • Vehicular collision confirmed — 1 case (2023-24). Infrastructure corridor risk identified and documented.
  • Inter-elephant conflict — 1 confirmed case in 2023-24, Dharamjaigarh, possibly musth-related.
  • Trend 2025-26 is critical — current year already shows 12 elephant deaths with months remaining in the financial year.

Laboratory Report Analysis

LAB COVERAGE

6 Lab Reports — 12 Animals Covered

Six batch reports from ICAR-IVRI Izatnagar cover 12 individual elephant deaths across the study period. Reports compiled under File F.2-19/DI/NRC/2025-26/CWL. Dr. M. Karikalan is the signing authority for all reports.

ARSENIC DETECTED

Arsenic in 2 Specimens — December 2025, Tamnar Range

Arsenic detected at elevated levels in visceral samples from 2 specimens from Tamnar Range (Raigarh Division), December 2025. Hepatocyte degeneration confirmed on histopathology. Indicative of contaminated water source or feed near industrial corridor. Environmental sampling of local water bodies recommended immediately.

CRITICAL GAP

Missing Report — ID 5008/2025

The laboratory report for specimen ID 5008/2025 is MISSING from the consolidated file. Both adjacent specimens (same batch, same location) showed arsenic positivity. This gap must be closed — the originating file should be obtained directly from ICAR-IVRI Izatnagar for completeness and medico-legal purposes.

TOXICOLOGY — NEGATIVE

Broad Toxicological Screen — 5 Batches

Five tested batches returned NEGATIVE results for: Nitrate-Nitrite compounds, Heavy metals (Pb, Hg, Cd), Organochlorine & organophosphate insecticides, Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN). This clears the most common pesticide/industrial toxin hypotheses.

EEHV — NEGATIVE

Elephant Endotheliotropic Herpesvirus (EEHV)

EEHV PCR testing returned NEGATIVE in all tested samples. No viral hemorrhagic disease involvement confirmed. This is particularly significant given the high neonate mortality — viral aetiology is ruled out for current cases.
