Workshop Context

Human-Elephant Conflict:
Data, Science & Solutions

This workshop brings together field officers, range managers, GIS specialists, and wildlife scientists from Dharamjaigarh and Raigarh Forest Divisions to review five years of incident data, examine postmortem and laboratory findings, and formulate a coordinated action plan for reducing human-elephant conflict casualties in Bilaspur district.

Data-driven decision making — translate 86 geo-referenced incidents into targeted infrastructure improvements
Cross-division coordination — establish real-time communication protocol between Dharamjaigarh and Raigarh mandals
Actionable intervention framework — prioritise electrocution prevention and drowning mitigation as immediate actions

Key Observation — Age Distribution in PM Cases

Neonates and calves (under 1 year) account for approximately 38% of PM cases. Multiple cases show calves found alone or separated from herd — suggesting herd disruption as a compounding factor. Three cases involve neonates with birth complications or asphyxia.

Data Quality Note

Some source records contain geographic coordinate anomalies (impossible DMS values) reproduced verbatim. One POR file (Report #13) is a seized meat examination report, not a death report — excluded from casualty count.

Slide 12 · PM Analysis

Key Findings from 32 PM Records

Sex distribution: 50/50 — 16 male / 16 female from 32 PM records. No significant sex-based vulnerability difference.
3 simultaneous electrocution events — Oct 2024 (3 elephants, Kachkoba), Mar 2026 (2 elephants, Ghargoda). Single illegal wire kills multiple animals.
6 neonate / calf cases — neonates under 30 days account for 19% of PM cases. Primary causes: asphyxia, birth complications, and drowning.
Vehicular collision confirmed — Dec 2023, Raigarh HQ, male ~15 yr. Cardio-respiratory failure due to chronic external injury on trunk from vehicle strike.
Raigarh 2024–26 surge — 10 of 18 Raigarh elephant deaths occurred in 2025-26 alone, nearly all in Ghargoda beat and Charmar/DehriDih localities.
