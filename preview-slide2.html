Objectives of Workshop
Biology, Behaviour & Disease

Build a comprehensive understanding of Chhattisgarh elephant population dynamics, anatomical orientation, and behavioural indicators — covering infectious diseases (EEHV, tuberculosis, anthrax) and non-infectious mortality causes including electrocution, train collisions, malicious poisoning, toxicosis, and disaster-induced events.

Necropsy, Biosafety & Sample Handling

Develop hands-on competency in field necropsy under biosafety and biosecurity protocols, vetro-legal investigation with chain of custody documentation, and rigorous sample collection, fixation, cold chain management, and transport logistics to reference laboratories.

Surveillance, Disposal & Remediation

Establish operational frameworks for active and passive health surveillance, implementing standardized protocols for safe carcass disposal, chemical site decontamination, and environmental remediation to protect local ecosystems.

Expected Outcomes
Field Necropsy Skills

Field veterinarians will be proficient in practical skills needed to properly position and systematically eviscerate heavy elephant carcasses in remote settings.

Sample & Lab Protocols

Hands-on experience with tissue fixative formulation and secure packaging to ensure diagnostic samples reach reference laboratories without degradation or cross-contamination.

Surveillance & Forensics

Implementation of field-validated carcass disposal and site decontamination protocols, standardized forensic reporting, and proactive health monitoring to rapidly detect emerging threats within the state’s elephant populations.
