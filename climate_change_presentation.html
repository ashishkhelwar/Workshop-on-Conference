Critical Thresholds

Five Tipping Points We Must Not Cross

07
🧊 West Antarctic Ice Sheet Collapse
Threshold: ~1.5°C

Irreversible collapse could raise sea levels by 3–5 metres over centuries, threatening coastal cities worldwide.

🌿 Amazon Dieback
Threshold: ~3–4°C or 25% deforestation

The Amazon flips from carbon sink to carbon source, releasing decades of stored carbon and destroying 40% of a critical biodiversity zone.

🌊 Atlantic Circulation (AMOC) Collapse
Threshold: uncertain — signs of weakening now

Would dramatically cool Northern Europe, intensify Sahel drought, and disrupt monsoon patterns across Asia and Africa.

❄️ Permafrost Thaw
Threshold: already occurring at current temperatures

1.7 trillion tonnes of carbon locked in Arctic permafrost. Sustained release creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop beyond human control.

🪸 Coral Reef Dissolution
Threshold: +1.5°C mass bleaching; +2°C = 99% loss

Reefs support 25% of all marine species and 1 billion people's food security. Already 50% of the Great Barrier Reef bleached since 1995.

Once triggered, these tipping points can cascade into each other — a "tipping cascade" that renders mitigation efforts irrelevant.

— Nature, Armstrong McKay et al., 2022

Next Steps

Five Actions That Move the Needle

13
01
Measure Your Emissions (Scope 1, 2 & 3)

Commission a full GHG inventory using GHG Protocol methodology — including supply chain (Scope 3). You cannot manage what you don't measure.

02
Set a Science-Based Target

Commit to SBTi 1.5°C-aligned reduction targets with interim milestones. Specify 2030 and 2035 waypoints — not vague net-zero pledges.

03
Switch to Renewable Energy Procurement

Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) make 100% renewable electricity cost-competitive in most markets. Act before policy forces the transition at higher cost.

04
Conduct Climate Risk Disclosure (TCFD/CSRD)

Get ahead of mandatory disclosure by voluntarily adopting TCFD framework — it surfaces risks not yet on the balance sheet.

05
Engage Your Supply Chain

Scope 3 accounts for 70–90% of total emissions. Supplier engagement, preferential procurement for low-carbon suppliers, and collaborative reduction programmes are essential.
